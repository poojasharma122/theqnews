Canadian NHL Teams 2025–26 Season Preview

A coast-to-coast breakdown of how Canada’s teams might fare this year

With seven Canadian teams battling for playoff spots, national pride, and long-awaited Cup glory, the 2025–26 NHL season is shaping up to be a defining one for hockey north of the border. From the high-powered Edmonton Oilers to the rebuilding Montreal Canadiens, here’s how we see each Canadian team performing this season:

1. Edmonton Oilers

2024–25 Finish: Western Conference Finalists

Prediction: Stanley Cup Final Contenders

Connor McDavid and Leon Draisaitl are still at the top of their game, and after another deep playoff run last season, Edmonton looks hungrier than ever. The addition of Isaac Howard and an improved bottom-six forward group has balanced their attack. If goaltender Stuart Skinner can stay consistent and the defense holds up, the Oilers could be Canada’s best shot at ending the Cup drought.

Key X-Factor: Defensive zone discipline

Projection: 1st in Pacific, Western Conference Finals or better

2. Toronto Maple Leafs

2024–25 Finish: Second Round Exit

Prediction: Unpredictable but dangerous

The Leafs made headlines by trading Mitch Marner, but they gained crucial cap flexibility and added defensive depth. Auston Matthews and William Nylander remain elite, and Ilya Samsonov has shown flashes of brilliance in net. The big question is: can this team finally get it done when it counts?

Key X-Factor: Postseason leadership and depth scoring

Projection: 2nd in Atlantic, Second Round exit

3. Vancouver Canucks

2024–25 Finish: Just missed playoffs

Prediction: Playoff Bubble Team

The Canucks have a high-end core (Pettersson, Hughes, Demko) and a coach in Rick Tocchet who’s stabilized the bench. If they can stay healthy and finally get consistency from their secondary scoring, Vancouver could squeak into the playoffs. They’ll be battling with LA and Seattle for the last wild card spot.

Key X-Factor: Goaltending consistency and health

Projection: 4th in Pacific, Wild Card hopeful

4. Calgary Flames

2024–25 Finish: Outside playoff picture

Prediction: Rebuild Mode

The Flames are in transition. With veterans like Kadri and Huberdeau aging and key players shipped out or on the block, Calgary’s focus is shifting toward youth. Their future looks brighter with prospects like Dustin Wolf and Jakob Pelletier, but don’t expect a serious playoff push just yet.

Key X-Factor: Development of young talent

Projection: 6th in Pacific, Outside playoffs

5. Ottawa Senators

2024–25 Finish: Bottom 5 in league

Prediction: Improving, but not quite ready

Ottawa has a strong young core: Tim Stützle, Brady Tkachuk, and Jake Sanderson are legit pieces. However, the Senators still lack veteran depth and have yet to figure out goaltending stability. They’ll win more games this year, but they’re probably a year away from truly contending.

Key X-Factor: Coaching, team defense

Projection: 6th in Atlantic, 80–85 points

6. Winnipeg Jets

2024–25 Finish: Just made playoffs

Prediction: Regressing without core stability

The Jets have taken a step back after losing key players like Nikolaj Ehlers in free agency. Questions remain around Connor Hellebuyck’s workload and leadership in the locker room. If Kyle Connor and Mark Scheifele can carry the offense and their defense holds, they could stay competitive—but a fall down the standings is likely.

Key X-Factor: Defensive depth and team morale

Projection: 5th in Central, fringe playoff contender

7. Montreal Canadiens

2024–25 Finish: Near bottom of East

Prediction: Still rebuilding, but exciting

Montreal’s youth movement continues, with Juraj Slafkovsky, Cole Caufield, and Kaiden Guhle leading the charge. They’ll be fun to watch and could play spoiler late in the season, but playoff contention is still at least two years away. Their real victory this year would be development and growth.

Key X-Factor: Progression of Slafkovsky and stability in net

Projection: 7th in Atlantic, ~75 points

Final Canadian Team Rankings (Projected)

  1. Edmonton Oilers – Elite contender

  2. Toronto Maple Leafs – Playoff lock, Cup hopeful

  3. Vancouver Canucks – On the bubble

  4. Winnipeg Jets – Hanging in

  5. Ottawa Senators – Improving, not there yet

  6. Calgary Flames – Early rebuild

  7. Montreal Canadiens – Rebuild phase
